The branding for the **Aurora** initiative draws inspiration from the whimsical and approachable style seen in the **Children’s Illustrated Guide to Kubernetes**, blending storytelling with cloud-native technology to make the platform more accessible and relatable. Our branding revolves around three unique characters and stunning vector backdrops, designed to represent the values of collaboration, innovation, and trust within the Government of Canada's cloud ecosystem.

## Characters

The Aurora initiative brings its story to life through three memorable characters such as Maple the Beaver, Nova the Loon, and Tundra the Wolf. Each character embodies the core values of the platform: collaboration, adaptability, and security.

## Visual Identity

Aurora’s visual identity is deeply connected to the values of the Canadian parliamentary system, using a range of symbolic imagery that reflects the platform’s commitment to governance, collaboration, and service to the public. Whether illustrating moments of clarity, unity, or resilience, these visuals reinforce Aurora’s role in supporting critical government operations and ensuring the continuous delivery of modern public services.

## Why Branding Matters

The Aurora initiative’s branding is designed to foster an approachable and inclusive atmosphere, making cloud-native concepts more digestible for all users, from developers to government decision-makers. The characters serve as a friendly entry point into understanding the complexities of Kubernetes, and other cloud-native environments, while the backdrops reflect the platform's steadfast presence in Canada's digital infrastructure.

This thoughtful combination of characters and visuals makes Aurora more than just a platform—it becomes a story, a journey, and a symbol of modern service delivery for Canadians.
